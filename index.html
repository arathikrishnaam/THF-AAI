What is Autism?

Neurological Variation

Autism is a complex neurodevelopmental condition characterized by unique ways of perceiving and interacting with the world.

Key Characteristics

  • Distinctive social communication patterns
  • Specialized interests and skills
  • Sensory processing differences

Global Perspective

Approximately 1 in 100 children worldwide are on the autism spectrum, with variations across different cultures and regions.

Dispelling Myths About Autism

Myth: Autism is a Disease

Fact: Autism is a neurological variation, not a disease. It's a different way of experiencing and understanding the world.

Myth: All Autistic People are the Same

Fact: The autism spectrum is incredibly diverse. Each individual has unique strengths, challenges, and experiences.

Myth: Autism Can Be "Cured"

Fact: Autism is a lifelong neurodevelopmental condition. Support, understanding, and acceptance are key.
